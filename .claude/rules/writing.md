# Writing rules

## Voice

Sound like a real founder wrote it. Sharp. Plain. Human. Class 9 English.

Anything that ships follows these rules:

- README files
- Docs (PRD, TDD, RESOURCES, runbooks)
- Landing copy
- App copy (labels, buttons, errors, tooltips)
- Judge facing copy (one pagers, slides, demo script)
- Blog posts, tweets, Farcaster posts
- Commit bodies
- Code comments
- Slack, Discord, partner emails

If you would not say it out loud to a teammate, do not put it on screen.

## Banned words

Do not use:

- delve
- unlock
- unleash
- robust
- leverage
- empower
- seamless
- harness
- streamline
- cutting-edge
- state-of-the-art
- revolutionize

If a banned word slips in, rewrite the sentence around the noun and verb. Usually you do not need an adjective at all.

## Banned phrases and patterns

- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "In the realm of"
- "Game changing"
- "Next generation"
- "Powerful, scalable, secure" (and any three adjective stack)
- "We are excited to announce"
- "We are proud to share"
- Em dashes used for drama (use a period or a comma)
- "Built with love" or any emoji laden tagline
- Sandwich opening (compliment → fact → call to action)

## Style

- Start with the noun that matters
- Say what the product does
- Prove claims with real behavior, numbers, or links
- Use short sentences
- Use plain words
- Cut filler words: very, actually, basically, just, really, simply, that, in order to
- Cut symmetric bullet pads (three bullets that say the same thing in different words)
- Cut adjective stacks

## Examples

Bad:

> Atrium is a revolutionary, next generation cross margin protocol that empowers users to seamlessly unlock the power of unified collateral across multiple venues.

Good:

> Atrium nets your collateral across venues. One wallet, one margin number.

Bad:

> In today's fast-paced world of decentralized finance, traders need a robust, scalable solution to streamline their cross venue exposure.

Good:

> Most traders post collateral twice when they hedge across venues. Atrium ends that.

Bad:

> We are excited to announce the launch of our cutting-edge formal verification framework.

Good:

> Plinth ships with five Kani-verified invariants in CI. The badge links to the proof run.

## Claims discipline

Every number on screen or in copy must be sourced.

- "Hyperliquid HIP-3 open interest peaked at $2.38B mid April 2026" → footnote the source (Yahoo Finance, The Defiant, on-chain query)
- "Collateral saved by 47%" → only if a real backtest published the number via `ResearchAttestation` on chain
- "5 Cohort partners" → only if Scribe shows 5 partners. Otherwise show the live count.

If a claim is not sourced, do not write it. Replace with "TBD pending [specific event]".

## Honesty patterns

When something is conditional, say so:

- "Robinhood Chain adapter ships within 14 days of the RH SDK going public."
- "Pyth equity feeds run on a Praetor signed relay until Pyth ships native Sepolia equity feeds."
- "If only 2 of 3 keepers are operational on judge day, the Lantern dashboard says 2 of 3."

When a scope cut happens, announce it the same day. Format:

> Cohort signup count was 3 of a target 5 by day 60. We are cutting press contact #2 outreach this week and reallocating F3 to BD. Next check in is day 75.

## Comments in code

Default: no comment. Names should carry the meaning.

Write a comment only when the why is not obvious from the code:

- A hidden invariant (`// margin_version must be incremented before queuing liquidation, see TDD §7.1 M6 race fix`)
- A workaround for a specific bug (`// IPyth.getPriceNoOlderThan reverts on 0-age stale checks; clamp to 1`)
- A surprising constant (`// 421614 is Arbitrum Sepolia chain id`)

Do not write comments that paraphrase the next line. Do not write `// generated by` or `// TODO maybe later`.

## Docs

PRD, TDD, RESOURCES, runbooks, and the design HTML files are the canon. When you write or update one:

- Lead with the version and last updated date
- Use tables for matrices, not paragraphs
- Cite resource file paths (`resources/<repo>/<file>:<line>`) when claiming a tool supports a behavior
- Keep section numbers stable so PRs and reviews can point at them
- When you change a claim, add an audit note explaining what changed and why (PRD §28 and TDD §24 are the precedents)

## Judge facing copy

The one pager, the deck, the demo script, the runbook. Per PRD §27 and §26.1.

- Open with a real person and a real number (Jamie + collateral gap)
- Show the proof in 90 seconds (Verifier Mode)
- Speak in present tense only about things that are actually live
- Close with three concrete asks

## Social and press

- One thought per post
- No tagline soup
- No emoji storms
- Numbers come with a link
- Names come with consent
- A founder voice, not a brand voice

## Final filter

Before publishing anything, ask:

- Does this sound like a real person?
- Would a builder reading this in 6 months still understand the why?
- Is every claim sourced?
- Did I use a banned word or pattern?
- Is the noun and verb doing the work, or am I leaning on adjectives?

If any answer is no, rewrite.
